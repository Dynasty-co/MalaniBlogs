Malani, Inc. is committed to effective environmental management as one of its

important corporate priorities, and will focus on the following initiatives:

  1. Compliance with all applicable environmental laws and regulations
  2. Measuring the impact of each of our operations on the environment will be systematically assessed for compliance with appropriately defined standards and reviewed periodically to mitigate or eliminate such impact.
  3. Improvement of employee environmental awareness and performance through detailed policies and procedures, training, and recognition of excellence.
  4. Measurement of environmental performance through auditing with employee accountability and reporting to senior management.

Gold Sourcing Policy

Malani, Inc. is concerned about the environmental and social impacts of irresponsible mining. We at Malani, Inc. will ensure that all our gold suppliers comply with internationally known gold sourcing guidelines. Furthermore, we are committed to ensure that our sourcing of gold, precious metals products, and articles are under the highest Social, Human Right and Environmental standard of trade such as Dodd Frank and Signet SRSP.

Synthetic Diamonds

Malani, Inc. is committed to the fight against undisclosed synthetic diamonds. The following methodology has to be adopted for ensuring compliance to undisclosed synthetic diamonds:

  1. Access to effective detection system
  2. Buying from trusted suppliers
  3. Reporting of undisclosed synthetics to supplier and interested parties whenever detected
  4. Recording the incidents of contamination reported and implementing suitable corrective and preventive measures for effective controls
  5. Classifying contamination of points into different category (High, Medium & Low)
  6. Identify the policy, procedure and test mechanism to implement the test mechanism in the organization.
  7. Necessary declaration of compliance is obtained from the supply chain and chain of assurance is passed on to the customers by printing synthetic declarations on all our invoices.

Disclosure of Treated Diamonds, Synthetics and Stimulant

The following essential principles will be applicable in all the transactions involving treated diamonds, synthetics and stimulant.

  1. Full disclosure i.e. the complete and total release of all available information about a Diamond and all material steps it has undergone prior to sale to the purchaser, irrespective of whether or not the information is specifically requested and regardless of the effect on the value of the diamond.
  2. No misuse of terminology or mis-representations or attempts to disguise the product will be made in the selling, advertising and distribution of treated diamonds, synthetics and simulant.
  3. The word ‘diamond’ will not be used in the case of names of firms, manufacturers or trademarks; in connection with treated diamonds or diamond simulant or synthetic diamonds.
  4. Compliance with MAP of De Beers in the entire supply chain and ensure trust of retail customers is maintained.

Kimberley Process and System of Warranties

Malani, Inc. is fully committed in complying with all the requirements specified in the Kimberley Process Certification Scheme and World Diamond Council’s (WDC) System of Warranties Declaration. The definition of ‘Conflict Gem Stone Diamonds’ as agreed by the Kimberley Process has been adopted and a declaration is received from all manufacturing sites and suppliers to ensure these requirements are upheld.

Health and Safety

Malani, Inc. recognizes the need to develop a sustainable, value-creating business and is committed to the following:

  1. Any adverse impact of our business processes on those who carry it out shall be identified and eliminated. Towards this end, we will systematically review our operations to identify sources of health and safety-related risks.
  2. This review will use appropriate standards as required by prevailing laws, expert opinion, and our knowledge of best practices.
  3. The review will lead to the formulation of clearly described work practices and drills.
  4. All our staff will be trained in the manner required to adhere to these work practices and drills.
  5. The health of our staff, exposed to certain hazardous processes, will be monitored periodically through appropriate medical checks, and reviewed using expert inputs for improvements.
  6. Workers shall not be under the influence of or abusing, drugs, alcohol, and/ or other illegal substances.
  7. All workplaces will be constructed to meet safety standards with local regulations as the minimum standards that will be applicable.
