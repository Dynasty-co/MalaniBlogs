The Magic of Meenakari art

The inspiration behind Navaratna Meenakari work in jewelry comes from two vast horizons of history. The word 'Navaratna' is derived from two Sanskrit words 'Nava' means 'Nine' and 'Ratna' means 'Gemstones'. It acknowledges the significance of southeastern Asian culture that denotes emblems of wealth, prestige, and prodigy. It adheres to a protective power for health benefits and well-being. This art portrays an interplay of astrology and cosmology. If you believe in astrology then you can customize your gemstone jewelry from the Malanis' based on your zodiac sign. The cultural background of the Meenakari artwork connects its dots from the Persian word 'Minoo' a feminine form of heaven and the word 'Meena' states the meaning of an azuring color of the sky. It depicts an amazing arena of the Mughal empire and elaborates on the patterns and motifs that are highly inspired by the great architecture of Persia. Wear an incredible gemstone meenakari choker set with a saree or with an ethnic indo-western outfit, it turns out to be the best look of yours. Two categories of meenakari jewelry; popularly known as "Ek rang khula" and "Panchrangi Meena" set a rush in demand in the jewelry industry. A single color of enamel is carved for the entire design in the first category and five colors are pearl white, powder blue, ink blue, ruby red & forest green crafted in the second. From Mongols to Indians this design has shown its prominence for centuries among jewelry lovers. Especially, women get smitten by the regalia of Meenakari artistry.
